Who We Are

Run By Members, Backed By Adults

Venturing is the Boy Scouts of America's program for older youth, built around high adventure, leadership, and a crew government that members actually run.

Crew 2140 was chartered by our sponsoring organization to give teens a program that treats them like the capable adults they're becoming. Every trip, budget line, and safety plan starts with youth officers — our adult Advisors coach from the sideline instead of running the show.

We meet twice a month during the school year to plan logistics, train on outdoor skills, and vote on where we're headed next. Then we go do it — rain, shine, or occasional minor snow event.

  • STRUCTURE
    Youth OfficersPresident, VP of Program, VP of Administration, Guide, and committee chairs elected each year by the crew.
  • SUPPORT
    Adult AdvisorsCertified in Youth Protection Training and outdoor safety, present on every trip, quiet in every meeting.
  • STANDARD
    Safety FirstEvery high adventure trip follows BSA's Guide to Safe Scouting and Tour & Activity Plans, no exceptions.
  • OPEN TO
    Everyone 14–20Co-ed, no Scouting background required — just a willingness to carry your own pack.
What We Do

Trips We Actually Run

A sample of the program — the exact lineup shifts every year based on what the crew votes for.

Backpacking

Multi-day treks on regional trail systems — trip planning, food-pack prep, and Leave No Trace practice built in.

Whitewater Rafting & Kayaking

Guided river days that build toward crew-led paddling once everyone's checked off swim and safety skills.

Rock Climbing & Rappelling

Top-rope days at a local crag or gym, run by climb-certified youth instructors and Advisors.

Winter Camping

Cold-weather shelter building and layering clinics, capped by an overnight snow camp for those who are ready.

National High Adventure Bases

Crew fundraises toward a flagship trip — Philmont, Sea Base, or Northern Tier — every two to three years.

Leadership & Awards

Officer training, the Venturing award trail, and mentorship for anyone chasing the Summit Award.

Get Involved

How To Join

It's a short path from curious to card-carrying crew member.

1

Reach out

Send us a message using the contact form or email below — tell us your age and what you're hoping to do outdoors.

2

Visit a meeting

Come to a regular meeting or a New Member Night as a guest, no paperwork or payment required for your first visit.

3

Complete the application

Fill out the BSA Youth Application and, if you're a parent joining as an Advisor, the Adult Application plus Youth Protection Training.

4

Pay dues & gear up

Annual registration plus crew dues covers BSA membership and insurance. We'll walk you through what gear you actually need to start.

Eligibility

  • Age 14 (and completed 8th grade) through 20 years old.
  • Open to all genders — no prior Scouting experience needed.
  • Parents and other adults 21+ are welcome to register as Advisors.
